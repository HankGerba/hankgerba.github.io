Hank Gerba

Updated November 2024

Profile

Hank is a media theorist specializing in the humanist study of computation, AI, and aesthetics. They received their PhD from Stanford University's Film & Media Program in the Art & Art History Department. Their dissertation, "Digital Disruptions: Moiré, Aliasing, and the Stroboscopic Effect"https://searchworks.stanford.edu/view/in00000106660 constructs a digital aesthetics founded on oscillations between analog and digital processes. Their work has been published in Real Life and Media-N, and is forthcoming elsewhere.

Outside of academia, Hank has nearly a decade of experience exploring the bleeding edge of technology with the Entertainment Technology Center (ETC) at the University of Southern California, a think tank which is tasked by major entertainment studios to look around the corner. At ETC, Hank co-produced the Emmy-nominated VR Short Wonder Buffalo: The VR Experience,https://vimeo.com/224432295 wrote for, operated, and programmed Sophia the Robot,https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sophia has written multiple white papers concerning the relationship between AI and narrative for studio CTO's, and is currently Lead Researcher of the "Universal Character Model" project, sponsored by Amazon Studios, Disney, NBCUniversal, Epic Games and Dolby. The project explores the forefront of AI's relationship to narrative structure and storytelling.https://cinema.usc.edu/etc/
